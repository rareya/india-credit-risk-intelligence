# Problem Statement: Credit Risk Intelligence for Emerging Indian Markets

## 🏢 Business Context
India is currently witnessing a massive credit expansion, yet **$530B in credit demand remains unmet** in the MSME sector. Traditional scoring models fail because they rely on historical formal credit data, which 60% of new-to-credit (NTC) borrowers in India lack. 

For a lender, the challenge is binary: **How do we approve more loans without increasing the Non-Performing Asset (NPA) ratio?**

## 📉 The Problem
Current manual and rule-based underwriting systems are:
1. **Inefficient:** High turnaround time (TAT) leads to customer drop-offs.
2. **Biased/Incomplete:** Relying on thin-file data leads to high rejection of credit-worthy individuals.
3. **Reactive:** Systems fail to detect early warning signals (EWS) before a default occurs.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
This project develops an automated **Credit Risk Intelligence Engine** to:
- **Classify Risk:** Predict the Probability of Default (PD) for loan applicants with >90% precision.
- **Optimize Thresholds:** Balance the trade-off between *Loss of Opportunity* (rejecting good clients) and *Credit Loss* (approving bad ones).
- **Regulatory Transparency:** Implement Explainable AI (SHAP/LIME) to provide "Reason Codes" for every credit decision, meeting RBI compliance standards.

## 🚀 Key Deliverables
- **The Engine:** A machine learning pipeline handling class-imbalanced financial data.
- **Risk Dashboard:** A visualization of borrower personas and key risk drivers (e.g., Debt-to-Income, Payment History).
- **Business Impact:** A simulated ROI analysis showing how this model reduces default rates by an estimated 15-20% compared to baseline models.
